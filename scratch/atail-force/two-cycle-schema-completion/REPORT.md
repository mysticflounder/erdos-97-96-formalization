## Exact production theorem shape

For one fixed CCW boundary chart, the comparison relation has exactly two
constructors.  For `a < b < c < d` in that chart:

1. `diagonalEqSide` uses `c,d in row(a)` and produces
   `dist(b,c) < dist(b,d)`;
2. `diagonalEqLastSide` uses `a,d in row(c)` and produces
   `dist(a,b) < dist(b,d)`.

`false_of_two_cycle` requires a forward and backward comparison under the
**same** boundary function.  The endpoint terms may be identified by selected
row equalities.  A cyclic shift of the original boundary is legitimate by
`isCcwConvexPolygon_cyclicShift`, while injectivity and image are transported
by the two companion theorems above.

`detect_two_cycle.py` implements exactly this contract:

1. make one node for every unordered carrier distance term;
2. join the four incident distance terms of every selected row;
3. for every cyclic cut, enumerate every increasing quadruple and only the
   two production constructors above;
4. quotient comparison endpoints by the row-equality graph;
5. find every reverse pair of quotient edges;
6. extract explicit equality paths and the exact membership literals needed
   for the two constructor terms; and
7. record the active cyclic roles, dihedral canonical key, and every
   reflection-canonical linear-cut record accepted by the existing CNF bank
   format.

The search is exhaustive for identities generated by the selected-row
equalities of the supplied finite carrier.  The production theorem permits
arbitrary `value` equalities; equalities from additional live geometry not
represented by the row incidence data are outside this detector's scope.

## Why the 218 and 262 banks missed the theorem

Both banks are inventories of schemas mined from earlier CEGAR witnesses.
They were never closed under the semantic instances of
`false_of_two_cycle`.  Their transport format also treats a schema as a
linear increasing order plus reflection.  It does **not** automatically add

## Finite canonical family verdict

There are two different answers depending on what “family” means:

1. **Abstract theorem motif: yes, one `n`-independent normal form.**
   Build the selected-row equality quotient of unordered distance terms and
   look for a directed two-cycle in the production comparison relation.  This
   statement is uniform in the carrier size and is the right generic
   consumer/detector boundary.

2. **Literal transported membership schemas: no finite `n`-independent list
   is justified.**  The equalities identifying the two comparison endpoints
   can travel through selected-row paths of unbounded length.  Each longer
   path introduces more centers, points, and boundary gaps.  Nothing in the
   current live hypotheses bounds those paths by six, seven, eight, or any
   other constant.

Therefore the safe architecture is an `n`-dependent occurrence/separation
search, or a formal quotient-cycle producer carrying its equality paths.  The
292-schema checkpoint is complete only for the two saved carriers and the
cyclic/reflection transports of their detected witnesses.  It is not a finite
all-`n` theorem bank and must not be presented as uniform live coverage.

## Route consequence

Before doing another geometric or LRA refinement on a finite carrier, run the
theorem-faithful quotient detector and close all cyclic-cut orbits of every hit.
That removes witnesses already contradicted by production Lean and prevents
re-mining the same mathematics under a different boundary cut.

For actual source closure, one additional theorem is still necessary:

```text
arbitrary live whole-carrier surface
  -> a quotient two-cycle (with explicit equality paths)
     or another proved geometric terminal.
```

The finite n=12/n=13 results do not prove that implication.  They improve the
CEGAR separation oracle and eliminate two saved outer carriers; they do not
close `DoubleApexOffSurplusSharedRadiusPair` or any LIVE-Q/C source hole.
