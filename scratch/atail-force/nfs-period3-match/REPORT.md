### 3. Lemma 266 / `K11`: sound one-cell necessary inequality

The newest verified ledger proves the following conditional statement in
prose and exact arithmetic; the snapshot does not contain it as a promoted
Lean declaration.  If
four named roles have cyclic order

```text
nI < x1 < c0 < b1
```

and the row centered at `b1` has the equality

```text
dist b1 x1 = dist b1 c1,
```

then strict Kalmanson gives

```text
dist nI c0 + dist c1 b1 > dist nI b1.  (K11)
```

This derivation is mathematically sound.  It is also only a necessary
inequality, not its contradictory opposite.

It does not match the full production all-reverse surface unconditionally:

- the production shared-cap-order packet orders physical-cap sources and
  their internal blocker centers;
- `x1` is one of the two targets outside the physical cap; and
- no current theorem places that outside target in the displayed global
  boundary interval for every identity/order branch.

Even in a branch where that order is established, `(K11)` supplies only the
forward strict inequality.  The opposite inequality needed for `False` was
observed in twenty explicit rational packets but was not proved from the
live hypotheses.

**Verdict:** sound regression/filter lemma for one role cell.  It neither
produces a co-radial pair nor closes the period-three residual.

## Final routing decision

No theorem in the newest NFS snapshot is the missing producer.  The only
NFS theorem worth considering for promotion is the first-apex shell-role
extractor, and only as supporting data inside an occurrence proof that also
uses the actual total transition.  Promoting it without such a consumer
would add plumbing but would not advance the source `sorry` count.

The next theorem must remain on the full-parent surface and prove one of:

1. one `transitionReverseOutsidePair T q` is co-radial from
   `S.oppApex1`;
2. one such pair has a second distinct center in the physical cap, with the
   critical/deletion hypotheses needed to produce the corresponding row; or
3. direct `False` from the full `L`, total transition, all-reverse relation,
   and shared cap order.

`K11` can serve as a branch-local necessary inequality or a regression gate
inside that proof, but it should not replace the aggregate occurrence target.

No production `sorry` was closed by this audit.
