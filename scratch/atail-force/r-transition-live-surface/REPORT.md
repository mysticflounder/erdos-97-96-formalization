## Verdict

The proposed two-field producer

```text
source blocker lies in surplusCap
+
mate lies in the source selected support
```

is not the weakest R target.  It is already an inconsistent terminal packet.
`RTransitionLiveSurface.lean` proves that robust survival at both opposite
apices excludes both endpoints of the closed surplus cap.  Therefore, if the
source blocker is in `surplusCap`, it is actually in
`capInteriorByIndex surplusIdx`.  Adding the directed cross hit turns the
blocker's exact shell into the selector-free packet

```lean

```

consisting only of a strict-surplus center, a global selected four-row at that
center, and membership of both robust-pair endpoints.  Ordered-cap
outside-pair uniqueness refutes this packet directly.  Critical-shell
provenance is not used by the terminal consumer.

Thus trying to prove both source-blocker fields is equivalent to trying to
close the branch already.  The smaller load-bearing target remains the
selector-free row capture, matching
`StrictSurplusRowCapturesErasedPair` in the double-survival normal-form audit.

endpoint in strict `oppCap1`.  For that orientation and every retained


```text

  AND source blocker outside surplusCap

or

```

The second equivalence is the production theorem
`cross_deletion_survives_iff_not_mem_selected_support`.  The first implication
uses the strict-surplus row-capture contradiction above.  The split is exact
with respect to the directed membership decision: the arms are generated by
`mate in support` versus `mate not in support`.

`sourceTransition_exact_split_under_live_outer_surface` restates the same
checked split after adding a second-large-cap hypothesis and the live
no-`IsM44` hypothesis.  `D` already carries global K4 and convexity; `S`
already carries the promoted MEC/cap packet.  The proof does not consume the
two added outer hypotheses.  This is not a proof that no stronger theorem can
use them; it records that the current bank supplies no transition from those
outer fields to either missing incidence decision.
