- `case_common` — bit holds in every exact completion of the leaf's case;

  elimination along the path (validated below).

Full bit list with per-class use counts is in the machine report under
`summary.required_bits.bits`.

## split_path semantics (INFERRED, then validated)

INFERRED semantics: each certificate leaf belongs to a case
`(relaxed row, forced separator pair)`; `case_id` is `R0xx:<pair>` with
pair `u=v`, `u=s1`, or `no_separator` (incidence census records the pair
as null for `no_separator`). The case starts from the exact completions of
the relaxed row carrying that forced pair (per-completion full masks in the
incidence census `rows[].exact_rows`). `split_path` is the list of binary
decisions from the case root to the leaf: entry `{center: c, label: l,
decision: yes|no}` keeps exactly the completions with `l ∈ mask(c)` (yes)
or `l ∉ mask(c)` (no). The leaf id is the relaxed id followed by the
path's decisions encoded Y/N in order.

Validated consequences (EMPIRICAL, all 135 leaves):

- Filtering the case's completions by the path leaves exactly the leaf's
  `exact_pids` — a single pid at every leaf; leaf count per case equals
  the case's completion count (135 leaves ↔ 135 completions).
- The leaf's `common_erased_masks` equals that surviving completion's
  masks on the 8 erased centers (members and mask integers), i.e. every
  membership bit is fixed at the leaf.
- The two exact centers v, w have case-level masks (`relaxed_masks` of the
  incidence row); every completion agrees with them, and all exact-center
  generator witnesses lie inside them.
- Mask integers (leaf certificates and incidence census alike) are
  bitmasks over the canonical label order ['u', 'v', 'w', 's1', 's2', 's3', 'Pw', 'Pu', 'Q1', 'Q2']
  (bit i = label i). A leaf's `support.centers_used` is the set of centers
  of its nonzero generators, listed in canonical order.

Examples:

- `R000:u=v:R000` (file `R000_ueqv_R000.json`): split_path = [],
  leaf suffix `(none)`, surviving exact pid `s1_002`.
- `R001:no_separator:R001N` (file `R001_no_separator_R001N.json`): split_path = [u:s1=N],
  leaf suffix `N`, surviving exact pid `s1_001`.
- `R001:no_separator:R001Y` (file `R001_no_separator_R001Y.json`): split_path = [u:s1=Y],
  leaf suffix `Y`, surviving exact pid `s1_000`.
- `R002:u=v:R002NNNN` (file `R002_ueqv_R002NNNN.json`): split_path = [s1:Pw=N, s1:w=N, u:Pw=N, Pu:s2=N],
  leaf suffix `NNNN`, surviving exact pid `s1_011`.
- `R009:u=v:R009NNYNNY` (file `R009_ueqv_R009NNYNNY.json`): split_path = [Pw:Q1=N, s1:w=N, s3:u=Y, u:w=N, s2:w=N, Q2:v=Y],
  leaf suffix `NNYNNY`, surviving exact pid `s2_024`.
- `R009:u=s1:R009Y` (file `R009_ueqs1_R009Y.json`): split_path = [u:Pw=Y],
  leaf suffix `Y`, surviving exact pid `s2_030`.

The R001 pair shows one split separating the case's two completions on the
bit `s1 ∈ mask(u)`: the `N` leaf's `common_erased_masks[u].members` excludes
s1, the `Y` leaf's includes it, and each leaf pins a different exact pid.

Structural observation (EMPIRICAL): per leaf, the 24 optional generators
(`source = leaf_common_submask`) are 3 per erased center, pairing the
center's first mask member (members lists are stored in canonical label
order, validated) with each of the other three; violations: 0.
The 6 required distance generators (`source = exact_center`) are 3 per
exact center (v, w); the optional flag equals `source == leaf_common_submask`
on every generator (deviations would be listed under anomalies).
